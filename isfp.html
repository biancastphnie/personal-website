two types of isfp people:

  • the two types of identities that people would identify as is either assertive or turbulent
  • i identify as a turbulent isfp (isfp-t):
    • turbulent isfp people are less adventurous than assertive isfp people
    • turbulent isfp people are more sensitive to stress and are more likely to feel overwhelmed than assertive isfp people
    • turbulent isfp people are more dependent on what other people think than assertive isfp people
    • turbulent isfp people are more likely better team sports than assertive isfp people

what isfp stands for:

  • mind -> determines how the person interacts with their environment -> (i) introverted
  • energy -> shows where the person directs their mental energy -> (s) observant
  • nature -> determines how the person makes decisions and cope with emotions -> (f) feeling
  • tactics -> reflects the person's approach to work, planning, and decision making -> (p) prospecting
